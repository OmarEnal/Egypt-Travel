About Cairo

Cairo, the capital of Egypt, is a vibrant metropolis with a rich tapestry of history spanning thousands of years. Known as "The City of a Thousand Minarets," Cairo's skyline is punctuated by countless elegant Islamic monuments that date back centuries.

At the heart of Cairo's allure are the ancient pyramids of Giza and the enigmatic Sphinx, standing as testament to the extraordinary achievements of ancient Egyptian civilization. These iconic structures, built over 4,500 years ago, continue to captivate visitors from around the globe.

Beyond the ancient wonders, Cairo offers a sensory feast of experiences.

The bustling Khan El Khalili bazaar invites you to wander through labyrinthine alleys filled with treasures, from intricate metalwork to aromatic spices. The Egyptian Museum houses an unparalleled collection of ancient artifacts, including the treasures of Tutankhamun.

Cairo blends the ancient and modern in fascinating ways, with luxury hotels and contemporary restaurants standing alongside historic Islamic and Coptic monuments. The flowing Nile River provides a serene counterpoint to the city's energetic pace, offering felucca rides at sunset and elegant riverside dining.
